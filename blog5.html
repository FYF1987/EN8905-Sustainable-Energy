Practical Activity:
Solar System Sizing & Home Energy Audit

Real engineering calculations linking solar theory to a Bahrain household

Posted: 3 July 2026  ·  Fahad Yousif Buradah

Stage 3 — Supporting Practical Activity

Activity 1: Home Energy Audit

Method

I conducted a 24-hour energy audit of 8 circuits in my home using a clamp meter (Fluke 376 FC), measuring real power consumption of each load category.

Annual consumption: 158.2 × 365 = 57,743 kWh/yr = 57.7 MWh/yr

Activity 2: Solar Array Sizing Calculation

Solar Array Sizing Formula:
Required Array Size (kWp) = Annual Energy (kWh) ÷ (PSH × 365 × System Efficiency)

Step 1 — Input data:
Annual energy = 57,743 kWh/yr · PSH = 5.5 hrs/day · System efficiency = 0.80 (inverter + wiring + soiling losses)

Step 2 — Calculate array size:
Array = 57,743 ÷ (5.5 × 365 × 0.80) = 57,743 ÷ 1,606 = 35.96 kWp ≈ 36 kWp

Step 3 — Number of panels (550 Wp monocrystalline):
Panels = 36,000 ÷ 550 = 65 panels

Step 4 — Estimated payback period:
System cost: 36 kWp × BD 450/kWp = BD 16,200
Annual saving: 57,743 × BD 0.025/kWh = BD 1,444/yr
Simple payback = 16,200 ÷ 1,444 = 11.2 years (with subsidies: ~7.5 years)

Step 5 — CO₂ avoided vs. fossil grid (490 g/kWh):
CO₂ avoided = 57,743 × 0.490 = 28,294 kg = 28.3 tCO₂/yr
