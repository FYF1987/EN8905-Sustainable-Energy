Practical Activity:
Home Energy Audit & Solar System Sizing

Real engineering calculations linking solar theory to a Bahrain household

Posted: 3 July 2026  ·  Fahad Yousif Buradah

Stage 3 — Supporting Practical Activity

Activity 1: Home Energy Audit

Method

I conducted a 24-hour energy audit of 8 circuits in my home using a clamp meter, measuring the real power drawn by each load category.

Annual consumption: 158.2 × 365 = 57,743 kWh/yr = 57.7 MWh/yr

Activity 2: Solar Array Sizing Calculation

Solar Array Sizing Formula:
Required Array Size (kWp) = Annual Energy (kWh) ÷ (Peak Sun Hours × 365 × System Efficiency)

Step 1 – Input data:
Annual energy = 57,743 kWh/yr · Peak Sun Hours ≈ 5–5.5 hrs/day (consistent with Bahrain’s measured GHI of ~2,150 kWh/m²/yr) · System efficiency = 0.80 (inverter + wiring + soiling losses)

Step 2 – Calculate array size:
Array = 57,743 ÷ (5.5 × 365 × 0.80) = 57,743 ÷ 1,606 = 35.96 kWp ≈ 36 kWp

Step 3 – Number of panels (550 Wp monocrystalline):
Panels = 36,000 ÷ 550 = 65 panels

Step 4 – CO₂ avoided vs. the gas-fired grid (~490 g/kWh):
CO₂ avoided = 57,743 × 0.490 = 28,294 kg ≈ 28.3 tCO₂/yr
On cost and payback – a deliberate omission: The original draft of this blog quoted a specific system cost (BD 450/kWp), a subsidy rate, and an 11.2-year payback period.
