Stage 2 — Current Energy System Assessment

Bahrain's Fossil-Fuel Grid

Prior to Al Dur, Bahrain's grid was 100% fossil-fuel powered — a mix of natural gas CCGT plants and older oil-fired steam turbines. Even today, approximately 70% of generation remains fossil-fuel based, making it one of the most carbon-intensive grids per capita in the world.

Technical Strengths of Fossil-Fuel Power

  • Despatchability: Can ramp up within minutes to meet peak summer AC demand in Bahrain
  • High capacity factor (85–92%): Generates continuously regardless of weather
  • Grid inertia: Synchronous generators stabilise grid frequency (50 Hz)
  • Proven infrastructure: Existing plants, pipelines and grid connections already in place
