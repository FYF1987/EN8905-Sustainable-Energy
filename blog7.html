Social Awareness Activity:
Explaining Solar Energy to SABIC Colleagues

30-minute session, 10 participants, 5-question quiz, and key findings

Posted: 9 July 2026  ·  Fahad Yousif Buradah

Stage 5 — Social Awareness Activity

Activity Overview

I organised a 30-minute session with 10 colleagues from SABIC's ICS, Electrical, and HSE departments to explain SDG 7, the case for solar PV, and the energy transition trade-offs in accessible, non-technical language.

Key Analogies Used

  • Solar panel = a tree that grows money: High upfront planting cost, but produces free fruit (electricity) for 25 years. A gas plant = buying fruit from a shop every day — forever dependent on the shop's price.
  • Capacity factor: "A solar panel works like a Bahraini employee — very productive during daylight hours (5.5 hrs peak) but needs rest at night. A battery is like a savings account — stores the day's earnings for the night."
  • 45 vs 490 g CO₂/kWh: "Imagine every unit of electricity from gas emits the weight of a full can of fizzy drink in CO₂. Solar emits only the weight of a few grapes."

Ethics of the Activity

Consent: All 10 participants gave verbal consent; participation was voluntary.
Anonymity: Quiz answers collected without names — results aggregated only.
Safeguarding: All adult participants; no vulnerable groups involved.
Date & Location: 8 July 2026, SABIC ICS Meeting Room B, Jubail Industrial Complex.

Quiz Questions & Results

Q1. What does SDG 7 stand for?
Options: A) Safe Water B) Affordable Clean Energy C) Climate Action D) Zero Hunger
✓ Correct: B — Affordable Clean Energy | 10/10 correct (100%)
Q2. What is the LCOE of solar PV in Bahrain compared to natural gas?
Options: A) Solar costs 3× more B) Same cost C) Solar costs 3× less D) Solar is free
✓ Correct: C — Solar $0.022 vs Gas $0.068/kWh (3× cheaper) | 8/10 correct (80%)
Q3. What is the main challenge of solar PV compared to gas?
Options: A) Too expensive B) Only works in daylight C) Produces CO₂ D) Needs water to operate
✓ Correct: B — Intermittency / daylight only | 9/10 correct (90%)
Q4. How many Peak Sun Hours does Bahrain receive per day on average?
Options: A) 2 hours B) 3.5 hours C) 5.5 hours D) 8 hours
✓ Correct: C — 5.5 hrs/day | 7/10 correct (70%)
Q5. How much CO₂ does solar PV save vs. natural gas per kWh generated?
Options: A) 10% B) 50% C) 75% D) Over 90%
✓ Correct: D — 97% less (45 vs 490 g/kWh) | 6/10 correct (60%)
