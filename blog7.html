Social Awareness Activity:
Explaining Solar Energy to Colleagues

30-minute session, a small group of colleagues, 5-question quiz, and key findings

Posted: 9 July 2026  ·  Fahad Yousif Buradah

Stage 5 — Social Awareness Activity

Activity Overview

I organised a 30-minute session with a small group of colleagues to explain SDG 7, the case for solar PV, and the real trade-offs of an energy transition, in accessible, non-technical language.

Key Analogies Used

  • Solar panel = a tree that grows electricity: a high upfront planting cost, but free “fruit” (electricity) for 25+ years. A gas plant is like buying fruit from a shop every day – permanently dependent on the shop’s price.
  • Capacity factor: “A solar panel is highly productive during daylight hours, but needs a battery – like a savings account – to cover the night.”
  • ~490 vs ~41 g CO₂/kWh: “Every unit of electricity from gas emits roughly the weight of a golf ball in CO₂. Solar emits only about the weight of a grape.”

Ethics of the Activity

Consent: all participants gave verbal consent; participation was voluntary.
Anonymity: quiz answers were collected without names; results are aggregated only.
Safeguarding: all participants were adults; no vulnerable groups were involved.
Date: 8 July 2026.

Quiz Questions & Results

Q1. What does SDG 7 stand for?
Options: A) Safe Water   B) Affordable and Clean Energy   C) Climate Action   D) Zero Hunger
✓ Correct: B – Affordable and Clean Energy | 100% answered correctly
Q2. Roughly how does solar PV’s global average cost compare to fossil-fuel power (IRENA, 2024)?
Options: A) Solar costs more   B) About the same   C) Solar is about 40% cheaper   D) Solar is free
✓ Correct: C – roughly 41% cheaper than the least-cost new fossil plant | 80% answered correctly
Q3. What is the main technical challenge of solar PV compared to gas?
Options: A) Too expensive   B) Only generates in daylight   C) Produces more CO₂   D) Needs constant water supply
✓ Correct: B – intermittency / daylight-only generation | 90% answered correctly
Q4. Roughly how many hours of strong sunshine does Bahrain average per day?
Options: A) 2 hours   B) 3.5 hours   C) Over 10 hours of sunshine (peak sun ~5–5.5 hrs)   D) 24 hours
✓ Correct: C | 70% answered correctly
Q5. Roughly how much lower is solar PV’s lifecycle CO₂ per kWh compared to natural gas?
Options: A) About 10% lower   B) About 50% lower   C) About 75% lower   D) Over 90% lower
✓ Correct: D – roughly 41 vs. ~490 g/kWh, over 90% lower | 60% answered correctly

Stage 5 Complete: Average quiz score: 80% – a strong grasp of SDG 7 and solar energy concepts. The “tree that grows electricity” analogy was the most effective communication tool. Three colleagues expressed interest in rooftop solar for their own homes – a small but real, real-world outcome of the session.
