Understanding what SDG 7 demands from the global energy sector

Stage 1 — SDG Research

What is SDG 7?

SDG 7 – Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy for all – is one of the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals adopted in the 2030 Agenda. Energy is the thread that connects economic growth, social equity, and environmental sustainability.

Key Fact: The most recent joint Tracking SDG 7 report (IEA, IRENA, UN Statistics Division, World Bank and WHO, 2025) found 655 million people still lacked access to electricity, with progress far too slow to reach universal access by 2030. Solar PV – especially off-grid and mini-grid systems – is the fastest-growing part of the solution.

Global Sustainability Frameworks Alignment

  • Paris Agreement (2015): limiting warming requires rapid decarbonisation of the power sector, in which solar plays a central role
  • IRENA Renewable Power Generation Costs 2024: new utility-scale solar PV averaged $0.043/kWh globally – 41% cheaper than the cheapest new fossil-fuel plant, and 91% of all newly-commissioned renewable capacity undercut fossil fuel costs
  • Bahrain’s National Renewable Energy Action Plan (NREP): originally targeted 5% renewables by 2025 and 10% by 2035; this was doubled to a 20% by 2035 target under the Government Programme 2023–2026
Conclusion for Blog 1: SDG 7 demands a rapid scale-up of low-cost renewable capacity worldwide. For Bahrain, with a strong solar resource (see Blog 2) and a national target that has already been doubled once, solar PV is both a policy obligation and, increasingly, the cheaper engineering choice.
