Stage 1 — SDG Research

What is SDG 7?

SDG 7 – Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy for all — is one of the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals adopted in the 2030 Agenda. Energy is the golden thread that connects economic growth, social equity, and environmental sustainability.

Key Fact: As of 2024, 733 million people still lack access to electricity (IEA), and 2.4 billion rely on polluting fuels for cooking. Solar PV is the fastest-growing solution to both problems globally.

Global Sustainability Frameworks Alignment

  • Paris Agreement (2015): Limit warming to 1.5 °C — solar essential for energy sector decarbonisation
  • IEA Net Zero by 2050: Solar and wind supply 70% of power by 2050; solar alone ~40%
  • IRENA World Energy Transitions Outlook: Solar capacity must reach 18,000 GW by 2050
  • Bahrain NREP 2021: 20% renewables by 2035; Al Dur Solar Plant 1,500 MW already operational
Conclusion for Blog 1: SDG 7 directly demands rapid scale-up of solar energy. For Bahrain and MENA, with the world's highest solar irradiation (2,200–2,600 kWh/m²/yr), this is both an obligation and an economic opportunity.
