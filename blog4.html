How Utility-Scale Solar PV Works

Solar PV panels convert photons from sunlight into DC electricity via the photovoltaic effect. In utility-scale plants (like Al Dur), thousands of monocrystalline silicon panels are arranged in arrays, feeding DC power through string inverters or central inverters that convert it to grid-compatible 50 Hz AC. Modern bifacial panels capture reflected light from both faces, increasing yield by 10–15%.

Justified Engineering Judgement

Engineering Judgement: Solar PV is the superior choice for Bahrain under SDG 7. With the world's cheapest LCOE ($0.022/kWh), 97% lower CO₂ than gas, zero fuel cost exposure, and Bahrain's exceptional 5.5 PSH/day resource, solar is both economically and environmentally dominant. The intermittency challenge (daylight only) is addressed through a hybrid approach: solar for daytime baseload, BESS for evening peak, retaining one CCGT for overnight backup — transitioning to 80% solar by 2040.
