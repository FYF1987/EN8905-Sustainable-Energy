Assessing the Future Alternative:
Utility-Scale Solar PV for Bahrain

Technical, economic & environmental trade-offs – and a justified engineering judgement

Posted: 30 June 2026  ·  Fahad Yousif Buradah

Stage 2 — Future Energy System Assessment

How Utility-Scale Solar PV Works

Solar PV panels convert sunlight into DC electricity via the photovoltaic effect. In utility-scale plants, arrays of monocrystalline silicon panels feed DC power through inverters that convert it to grid-compatible AC. Bifacial panels, which capture reflected light from both faces, are increasingly standard in new utility-scale projects, including in the Gulf region.

Justified Engineering Judgement

Engineering Judgement: On cost and emissions, solar PV is now the stronger choice globally, and Bahrain’s solar resource (~2,150 kWh/m²/yr, >10 sunshine hours/day) is well above the world average. The genuine, unresolved challenge is reliability: a 17.4% capacity factor means solar alone cannot replace gas’s despatchable, 24/7 output. The credible near-term pathway – and the one EWA’s own Energy Transition Plan points toward – is a hybrid system: solar for daytime baseload, battery storage for evening peak, and gas retained as backup while its share is progressively reduced toward the 2040 target of >45% clean energy.
