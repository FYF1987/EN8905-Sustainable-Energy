Bahrain & MENA Solar Context:
The World's Best Solar Resource, Underused

Why Bahrain's geography makes solar its most powerful clean energy option

Posted: 23 June 2026  ·  Fahad Yousif Buradah

Stage 1 — Regional Energy Need

Bahrain's Solar Resource

Bahrain sits in one of the highest solar irradiation zones on Earth. With a Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) of approximately 2,200 kWh/m²/year and Peak Sun Hours (PSH) averaging 5.5 hours/day, the energy from sunlight falling on Bahrain's land area is vastly larger than its total electricity demand.

Al Dur Solar Plant — Bahrain's First Utility-Scale Solar

The Al Dur Solar PV IPP (Independent Power Producer) project — Bahrain's largest — became operational in 2024 with 1,500 MW capacity. Developed by a consortium including ACWA Power, it supplies approximately 30% of Bahrain's peak demand from zero-emission solar energy, directly contributing to SDG 7.2 targets.

Al Dur Key Facts: 1,500 MW capacity · 6 km² land area · 3.3 million solar panels · Powers ~115,000 homes · Avoids 2.2 million tCO₂/year · LCOE: ~$0.025/kWh
